What is Web Accessibility?

Web accessibility means creating websites and applications that can be used by everyone, including people with disabilities. It ensures equal access to information and functionality for all users, regardless of their abilities or the technologies they use.

Why Accessibility Matters

The impact of accessibility is substantial:

1.3B People worldwide with disabilities
15% Of global population
98% Of websites have accessibility issues

Key Accessibility Principles

Perceivable

Information must be presented in ways that users can perceive, including text alternatives for images and captions for videos.

Operable

Users must be able to navigate and interact with interfaces using various input methods, including keyboard-only navigation.

Understandable

Content and interface operations must be clear and easy to understand for all users.

Robust

Content must work reliably across different browsers, devices, and assistive technologies.
